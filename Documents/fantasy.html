Thoughts

I am a research assistant at the Bank of Canada. In this lifetime, I have been fortunate enough to research various topics using a tool-kit, I am lucky to be familiar with, which is statistics. Statistics helped me work on sociology, space engineering, healthcare analytics, applied and theoretical statistics, sports analytics, and now economics at the central bank of Canada. I am grateful for everyone who provided me these opportunities. I like exploring ideas and learning from different perspectives. I have goals, dreams, and questions that I want to answer. In this pursuit, I am willing to invest hours, days, years, my life, and perhaps, my mind. I like reading and listening podcasts about psychology, philosophy, and history. My favorite book is Meditations by Marcus Aurelius, and my favorite podcaster is Lex Fridman. My research interests are on LLM and causality. One of the questions that I particularly one to tackle is, "Can we replicate the human understanding or intelligence one models?", "Does the model need to be intelligent or convince humans to be intelligent, if we can not detect it does it matter? Does it matter if they are become concious or convincing us, humans that they are concious?" I am an easy person to reach out. From all disciplines, I would be happy, and grateful to be talk with you. Those who wonder, my name is pronounced as Four-cun. It's really not hard, just unfamiliar.

Projects

  • SSC 2025 - Case Study | Python - R
    • Case study for prediction of new onset atrial fibrillation using routinely reported 12-lead ECG variables and electronic health data.
    • Planning to implement a semi-parametric model. We are at the first stages of the study.
  • Traumatic Brain Injury | R
    • Studied post-traumatic brain injury patients to explore the impact on the volume of the left hippocampus (cognitive recovery).
    • Used a Non-Linear Mixed Model to analyze the relationship of the left hippocampus with demographic and tissue data.
    • Found that males experience a faster rate of decline in hippocampus volume compared to females after injury.
    • Detected a significant relationship between gray matter and hippocampus volume, suggesting that language and memory functions are also greatly affected.
  • Optimization Methods | R
    • Compared Newton-Raphson, Basic Monte Carlo, and Simulated Annealing optimization methods.
    • Found Simulated Annealing to be the most robust through a simulation study.
  • Soccer World Cup 2022 Data Analysis | R
    • Scraped and analyzed World Cup statistics to conduct exploratory data analysis.
    • Used Monte Carlo simulations to predict outcomes for standings and scores.
    • Visualized player performance metrics and calculated expected goals.
  • Airbnb Market Analysis | RShiny
    • Developed an interactive web application to analyze Airbnb market dynamics in Amsterdam.
    • Created an interactive map of Amsterdam, allowing users to filter and visualize room prices based on selected criteria.
  • Research Assistant | STEM Competition | York University (Jan 2024 - May 2024)
